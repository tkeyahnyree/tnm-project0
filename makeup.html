Drugstore Diva

Experimenting with liquid/pencil eyeliners and lip balm were my introduction to makeup back in middle school. I like to think my makeup journey truly started when I turned 18.
I got a job at an accessories boutique that encouraged glam through gratis as well as a generous discount. Excited to look the part- I rushed to my local drugstore, straight to the maybelline section, and grabbed what i thought to be the basics: bb cream, mascara, a double-sided eyeshadow stick, lip stain and eyeliner. Metallic smokey eyes with a bold red or burgundy lip became my uniform until fashion magazines told me otherwise.
Fast forward to 22 , I’ve been away from the boutique at least 2 years and am no longer immersed in trends. An opportunity with a world leading cosmetic company arises and I’m a newbie all over again. I had a strong, diverse team with solid cosmetic backgrounds to learn and exchange techniques with. My time here is what introduced me to incorporating higher end products into my beauty routine and birthed a hobby of staying plugged into the industry for good.

Quarantine Queen

Where am I going? I ask myself this question on two occasions: before/after I’ve planted myself at the vanity to put on a face, and while I’m lying in bed adding value sets to my cart. My first stay-home project was to downsize and de-clutter my space. Everything has a place and looks great. However, I noticed that I’ve slowly started replacing products instead of reducing them. I’m a sucker for a 30% or more discount coupled with free full sized items.
